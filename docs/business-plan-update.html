Section 2

The Problems Iteshō Solves

2.1 Problem 1: Payment Delays Are Existential Threats

📊 The Data: Payment delays are described as an existential threat to contractor viability — more damaging than a market downturn. Payment cycles have extended to 75 days or more in practice.

Why this happens:

  • Manual invoicing processes take weeks
  • Disputes over quantities and rates delay approval
  • Missing documentation requires back-and-forth
  • No audit trail means verification takes time

How Itesho solves it:

  • Auto-generates invoices from PM-approved quantities
  • Eliminates back-and-forth over documentation
  • Audit trail means verification takes minutes, not weeks
  • Contractors get paid faster

2.2 Problem 2: Disorganised Documentation Gets Paid Last

📊 The Data: Many delays start with the contractor's own documentation — evidence scattered across WhatsApp, email, Excel, and paper invites queries. Disorganised claims get paid last.

Why this happens:

  • Site teams use WhatsApp for daily reporting
  • BOQs live in Excel spreadsheets
  • Photos are on phones, not attached to work items
  • No link between site work and invoicing

How Itesho solves it:

  • Daily logs are BoQ-linked and audit-ready
  • All documentation is in one place
  • Photos are attached to work items with timestamps
  • Site work → PM approval → invoice in one flow

2.3 Problem 3: Avoidable Administrative Breakdowns

📊 The Data: Many disputes stem from avoidable administrative breakdowns rather than technical legal disagreements.

Why this happens:

  • No structured workflow for approvals
  • No audit trail for who approved what
  • Email and WhatsApp approvals get lost
  • "He said, she said" disputes

How Itesho solves it:

  • Structured PM approval workflow
  • Full audit trail of every action
  • No lost approvals
  • Disputes resolved with evidence, not arguments

2.4 Problem 4: Systemic Culture of Non-Compliance

📊 The Data: The BIBC exposed a systemic culture of noncompliance — between 2002 and 2024, only two contractors were blacklisted. The era of inaction is over, with 52 contractors now barred from doing business with the state.

Why this happens:

  • No enforcement of compliance before work starts
  • No tracking of compliance expiry dates
  • Contractors work without valid documentation
  • Claims denied due to non-compliance

How Itesho solves it:

  • Gatekeeper verifies compliance before work is submitted
  • Expiry date tracking with alerts
  • Blocks non-compliant contractors from submitting
  • Audit trail shows compliance status

2.5 Problem 5: 79% of Public Infrastructure Projects Delayed

📊 The Data: 79% of public infrastructure projects were delayed in the 2024/2025 financial year, with a major driver being contractors still relying on manual reporting and disjointed spreadsheets.

Why this happens:

  • Manual reporting is slow and error-prone
  • No real-time visibility into project status
  • Data is scattered across spreadsheets, email, WhatsApp
  • No integrated view of all projects

How Itesho solves it:

  • Real-time dashboard shows all projects
  • All data in one platform
  • Progress tracking linked to BOQ items
  • Integrated view of all projects

2.6 Problem 6: Government Mandate for Digitised Tracking

📊 The Data: The South African Construction Action Plan (SACAP) is now mandating digitised, integrated project tracking systems by March 2026 — requiring real-time tracking of every construction project including contractor, timeline, budget, and physical progress.

Why this matters:

  • Contractors who can't demonstrate digitised tracking won't win government work
  • Manual processes are no longer acceptable
  • SACAP is a regulatory requirement
  • Those who adopt digital tracking early have a competitive advantage

How Itesho solves it:

  • Itesho is already a digitised, integrated project tracking system
  • Compliant with SACAP requirements before the deadline
  • Real-time tracking of contractor, timeline, budget, and physical progress
  • Early adopters gain competitive advantage
