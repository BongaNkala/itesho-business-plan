Executive Summary

💡 The Opportunity: South Africa's construction industry loses 15-30% of project value to payment disputes. Itesho solves this by ensuring only compliant, Project Manager-approved work gets invoiced.

1.1 The Problem

  • 100,000+ construction companies in South Africa still manage BOQs with Excel and WhatsApp
  • 15-30% of project value is lost to payment disputes
  • No compliance verification gate before work is submitted
  • No structured audit trail for who approved what and when
  • Manual invoice calculations create errors, delays, and disputes

1.2 The Solution

Section 5

The Approval Chain

Itesho enforces a 4-step approval chain where Project Managers must certify all work before it becomes invoiceable.

🔒 Step 1
Compliance Gatekeeper
→
📋 Step 2
Daily Log Submitted
→
✅ Step 3
Project Manager Approves
→
💰 Step 4
Invoice Auto-Generated
🔒 The Golden Rule: No compliance → No daily log → No PM approval → No invoice.

5.1 Who Can Approve?

RoleAuthority LevelConfigurable
Project ManagerFull approval authorityPrimary approver
Construction ManagerApprove on behalf of PMYes
Site ManagerApprove within delegated scopeYes
Quantity SurveyorApprove quantities and ratesYes
Any Authorised RoleAs defined by client org structureFully configurable

5.2 The Compliance Gatekeeper — How It Works

  1. System checks compliance requirements per project (safety file, insurance certificates, permits, inductions)
  2. Blocks access to daily logs and submissions if any item is missing or expired
  3. Notifies users what's required to unlock the project
  4. Verifies & unlocks once all requirements are met
  5. Proactive alerts when certificates are approaching expiry

SWOT & Risk Mitigation

11.1 SWOT Analysis

💪 Strengths

  • Unique PM approval + compliance gatekeeper (no competitor has both)
  • B-BBEE Level 1 — competitive advantage for gov/enterprise
  • Cloud + desktop — serves all market segments
  • Founder: rare combination of construction domain knowledge AND technical capability
  • Lowest market price with most features (R1,500 vs R8,000+)
  • Capex-light SaaS model with 72%+ gross margins

⚠️ Weaknesses

  • New entrant with no brand equity
  • Limited marketing budget in early stages
  • Solo founder dependency
  • Early-stage — limited case studies and social proof
  • No mobile app yet (mobile web only)
  • Small team limits parallel initiatives

🚀 Opportunities

  • Government digital procurement drive (National Treasury directive)
  • 15-30% payment dispute problem crying out for solution
  • B-BBEE compliance requirements favouring Level 1 suppliers
  • Pan-African expansion (SADC region similar frameworks)
  • Smartphone penetration enabling mobile site logging

🔥 Threats

  • Established competitors (Procore, BuildSmart) — though they're expensive and less feature-complete for SA
  • Economic headwinds reducing construction spend
  • Regulatory changes in CIDB, COID, POPIA
  • New entrants copying the compliance gatekeeper concept
  • Rand volatility affecting cloud infrastructure costs (USD-priced services)
